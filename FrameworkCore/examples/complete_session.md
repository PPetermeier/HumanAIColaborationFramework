# Example: First Collaboration Session Using the Framework

---

**Session Context:**
> Goal: Outline the research questions for a new AI Ethics Paper.  
> Duration: ~45 minutes.

---

## Before the Session

- ✔ Defined clear objective: e.g draft 5 meaningful questions / actionable goals for your project.
- ✔ Reviewed Collaborator Profile & Project Foundations.
- ✔ Identified current knowledge gaps via the Living Questions document.

---

## During the Session

### Prompt Initiation

“Let's develop 5 potential [relevant thing] on [topic]. Please consider novelty, relevance, and ethical depth. I’ll critique each one.”

### Interaction Cycle

1. 🤖 AI suggests 5 questions + rationales.
2. 🧐 Human reviews, flags 2 as weak, requests deeper reframing.
3. 🤖 AI refines 2 flagged questions + suggests framing literature.

### Insights Logged

- AI proposed trend-driven questions, but lacked philosophical anchoring.
- Collaboration improved after explicit critique-feedback loop.

---

## After the Session

### Documentation

- Final set of 5 research questions confirmed.
- Notes on strengths & weaknesses in AI suggestion quality.
- Identified need for refining prompt specificity in future sessions.

### Reflection

- Observation: Precise framing = better AI output.
- Learning: Collaboration benefits from structured critique during sessions.

### Next Steps

- Create a reading list on the topic to deepen context for both human & AI.
- Document feedback patterns for future session setup.

---

**Session Outcome:**
 Collaboration quality improved through meta-collaboration and critique cycles.  
 Human learned more about guiding AI toward desired depth, understood potential of the framework and cooperation in general.
 AI adapted to critique, producing better output in second iteration.
